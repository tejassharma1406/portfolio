I am an early-stage venture capital analyst with hands-on experience across deal evaluation, financial due diligence, and fund operations. As a VC Analyst at Tech Launch Arizona, I conduct end-to-end diligence on IP-backed startups for the Wildcat Philanthropic Seed Fund — a $5M evergreen university fund — covering market analysis, competitive benchmarking, financial modeling, and Investment Committee presentations.

My evaluation work spans four sectors: digital health, mobility SaaS, aquaculture biotech, and deep tech semiconductor equipment. Investment Interests

Where I look for
breakout companies.

The sectors where I spend research time, track deal flow, and develop investment conviction.

⬡
Artificial Intelligence

Foundation model infrastructure, vertical AI applications, and agentic workflows that create durable competitive advantages. Focused on companies where AI is the core product — not a feature bolted on.

◈
Digital Health

Diagnostics, care delivery, and health data infrastructure. Attracted to platforms with defensible clinical validation, clear reimbursement pathways, and the potential to shift care from hospital to home.

◇
Fintech Infrastructure

Payment rails, embedded finance primitives, and B2B financial tooling. The most durable fintech businesses are those that become invisible infrastructure — high switching costs, network effects, and volume-driven unit economics.

▣
Developer Tools

Tooling that compounds developer productivity — CI/CD, observability, security, and AI-assisted coding workflows. Bottom-up adoption with usage-based pricing creates flywheel dynamics that are difficult to replicate.

◉
Frontier Technologies

Semiconductor equipment, advanced materials, climate tech, and space infrastructure. Long gestation, but IP-driven moats and government tailwinds (CHIPS Act, IRA) are creating investable entry points at Seed and Series A.
