# Driver Independence Mobility Platform

**Sector:** Mobility SaaS
**Stage:** Early Stage
**Evaluated:** 2025

---

## Problem

Gig economy drivers across rideshare and delivery platforms operate with limited visibility into cross-platform demand patterns, incentive structures, and earnings optimization strategies. Existing tools are either platform-specific or too narrow in scope to address the cross-platform coordination challenge, leaving a large workforce underserved.

## Solution

A cross-platform software tool aggregating demand signals and operational data across multiple rideshare and delivery networks, providing personalized real-time guidance to help drivers optimize when, where, and how they work. The platform is designed to improve earnings efficiency without requiring manual coordination across separate applications.

## Market Opportunity

The gig transportation workforce is large, growing, and largely underserved by purpose-built software. Driver-side tooling is emerging as a distinct software vertical with meaningful addressable market and limited cross-platform competition. The absence of a dominant player creates an early-mover opportunity for a company that can establish network effects before incumbents respond.

## Competitive Landscape

Existing solutions are narrow and platform-specific. No well-funded competitor has established a dominant cross-platform position. The medium-term risk is native feature development by rideshare platforms, which have strong incentives to shape driver behavior through their own interfaces.

## Evaluation Focus

The evaluation focused on unit economics structure, the sustainability of platform API access, driver retention and engagement cohort behavior, and the strategy for building a data position that becomes harder to replicate over time.

## Investment Perspective

Cross-platform tooling for gig workers addresses a clear and measurable pain point in a fragmented market. The central investment question is around defensibility: whether the company can build a dataset and network position that compounds before larger platforms respond with competing features or API restrictions.
