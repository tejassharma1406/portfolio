# Oral Vaccine Platform for Aquaculture

**Sector:** Aquaculture Biotech
**Stage:** Early Stage
**Evaluated:** 2025

---

## Problem

Disease management in commercial aquaculture relies heavily on injectable vaccines and antibiotic treatments. Injectable approaches require significant labor, cause fish handling stress, and face growing regulatory and consumer pressure around antibiotic use. These constraints limit operational scalability and create sustainability risks increasingly scrutinized by food retailers and regulators in major markets.

## Solution

An oral vaccine delivery system enabling mass immunization of farmed fish through feed-based administration. The platform is designed to eliminate manual injection, reduce fish handling stress, and enable disease prevention at a scale not feasible with current injectable methods.

## Market Opportunity

Aquaculture is among the fastest-growing protein production sectors globally. Disease prevention is consistently identified as a top operational priority by farm operators, and adoption of more scalable health management tools is accelerating as farm scale increases and sustainability mandates tighten.

## Competitive Landscape

Injectable vaccines from a small number of established veterinary pharmaceutical companies remain the standard of care. Oral delivery has historically faced technical barriers related to antigen stability and commercial-scale efficacy. A platform that credibly overcomes these barriers represents significant competitive differentiation.

## Evaluation Focus

The evaluation examined the biological mechanism and efficacy evidence, manufacturing scalability, the regulatory approval strategy across key markets, and the quality of existing relationships with commercial farm operators.

## Investment Perspective

An oral vaccine platform with demonstrated commercial-scale efficacy could reshape disease management across a large and growing industry. The key investment question is the degree to which technical and regulatory risk has been reduced relative to the company's current stage and capital requirements.
