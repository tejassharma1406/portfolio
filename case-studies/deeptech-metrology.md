# Optical Metrology System for Semiconductor Manufacturing

**Sector:** Deep Tech / Semiconductor Equipment
**Stage:** Growth Stage
**Evaluated:** 2025

---

## Problem

As semiconductor manufacturing advances to smaller process nodes, incumbent inspection and measurement systems face fundamental throughput and cost limitations. Traditional electron-based metrology approaches are struggling to scale economically at advanced nodes, creating yield management bottlenecks for leading-edge chip manufacturers.

## Solution

An optical metrology system designed for high-throughput wafer inspection at advanced semiconductor process nodes. The platform applies a proprietary optical detection approach to enable faster defect identification at a lower cost per measurement than incumbent scanning electron microscopy systems, without sacrificing the resolution required for advanced node process control.

## Market Opportunity

The semiconductor equipment market is large and strategically critical. Process control and metrology is a growing subsegment as chipmakers' yield requirements become more stringent with each node transition. Government manufacturing investment programs are creating meaningful near-term demand independent of broader semiconductor demand cycles.

## Competitive Landscape

The process control market is dominated by a small number of established equipment companies with deep installed bases. New entrants must demonstrate clear performance advantages and navigate multi-stage qualification processes spanning a year or more. The barrier to entry is high, but the performance ceiling of incumbent approaches at advanced nodes creates an opening for differentiated optical solutions.

## Evaluation Focus

The evaluation examined technology readiness level, depth of customer engagement, IP defensibility, manufacturing scalability, and the semiconductor industry experience of the founding team. Customer qualification status was treated as the primary near-term investment signal.

## Investment Perspective

Advanced metrology is a high-barrier, high-value category where technical differentiation translates into pricing power and long-term customer retention once qualification is achieved. Companies demonstrating credible performance at advanced nodes are well-positioned to benefit from both advanced node transitions and the structural increase in domestic semiconductor manufacturing investment.
